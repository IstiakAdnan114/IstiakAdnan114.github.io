Research & Publications

This research focused on developing an innovative IoT-based pill dispensing and packaging system. By integrating Arduino and ESP32, the system achieves real-time pill counting, precise weight measurement, and automated rejection of defective bottles, significantly improving accuracy and operational efficiency in pharmaceutical packaging.

Authors: Md. Istiak Adnan, Dr. John Doe

Journal: International Journal of Smart Systems, Vol. 5, Issue 2, 2025

View Publication →

This project automates pill dispensing and packaging in the pharmaceutical industry to minimize human error and improve efficiency. The system integrates IR sensors for pill counting and bottle detection, a load cell with HX711 for weight validation, servo motors for dispensing and rejection, and a DC motor-driven conveyor belt. An ESP32 microcontroller coordinates all components and transmits real-time data (pill count, bottle count, and weight) to a dedicated server for monitoring.

Course: IPE 419, Department of IPE, BUET

Supervisor: Dr. Nafis Ahmad

Team Members: Md. Rakonuzzaman, Md. Istiak Adnan, Md. Sheum Rahman, Toufiqur Rahman, M. Sakib Arman Saki, Iqbal Hossain Maruf
